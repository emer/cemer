MAJOR fixes to lesion code:
* Layer-level lesion of a built layer MUST call Lesion() on all of its units b/c MANY
routines enter directly at the unit level!  and unlesion for unlesion..
* Likewise, Unit-level Lesion must inactivate all of its con groups, b/c that is another direct
entry point.
* Both of the above were major oversights in existing code -- not sure how I missed this before
but it means that lesioned layers were likely learning and doing lots of unit-level processing --
prior to the threading re-write everything did go through the layer-level and so it would have
worked then, but not since the threading rewrite..  yikes!
* Moved the netin rescaling factors to LeabraPrjnState instead of ConState -- b/c it was
computed recv-side, then copied to senders, it means that effectively a single scale factor
was used for all projections, but not deterministically so... now it is deterministic, and we save on re-computing the scaling factor redundantly over-and-over again, and copying it out to the con groups.  not a major cost but anyway, this is much more sensible.  and it fixes a specific bug when the first unit in the layer was lesioned, as most keyed off of that one..
